    <levelledPara id="lp-0001">
      <para>To achieve the overall goal of the Dawn mission, a set of science objectives has been established that can be met by the instrument suite. These objectives are listed below.</para>
    </levelledPara>
    <levelledPara id="lp-0002">
      <para>The objectives primarily answered by the framing cameras are:</para>
      <sequentialList>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0001">To determine the origin and evolution of Vesta and Ceres by mapping the extent of geologic processes on the asteroid surfaces, and by using the cratering record to establish a relative chronology of the crustal units and population of impactors in the early solar system</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0002">To map the shape, determine the spin state, and establish the degree of cratering of the asteroids visited</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0003">To map the topography of Vesta and Ceres</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0004">To search for dust and satellites in the environment of the asteroids visited</listItem>
      </sequentialList>
    </levelledPara>
    <levelledPara id="lp-0003">
      <para>The framing cameras will also contribute to answering some of the broader objectives:</para>
      <sequentialList>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0005">To provide a geologic, compositional, and geophysical context for the Howardite-EucriteDiogenite (HED) meteorites.</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0006">To provide an opportunity to identify Ceres-derived meteorites in their geologic context.</listItem>
      </sequentialList>
    </levelledPara>
    <levelledPara id="lp-0004">
      <para>The visible and infrared spectrometer scientific objectives are:</para>
      <sequentialList>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0007">To provide a geologic, compositional, and geophysical context for HED meteorites</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0008">To provide an opportunity to identify Ceres-derived meteorites in their geologic context</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0009">To map the thermophysical properties of Vesta and Ceres</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0010">To determine the origin and evolution of Vesta and Ceres by mapping the mineralogical composition and its spatial variation across the asteroidal surface</listItem>
      </sequentialList>
    </levelledPara>
    <levelledPara id="lp-0005">
      <para>The gamma ray and neutron spectrometer scientific objectives are:</para>
      <sequentialList>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0011">To map the major elemental composition of O, Si, Fe, Mg, Ti, Al, Ca, and H on Vesta and Ceres</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0012">To map the trace elements U, Th, K, Gd, and Sm on Vesta and Ceres</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0013">To provide a geologic, compositional, and geophysical context for the HED meteorites</listItem>
      </sequentialList>
    </levelledPara>
    <levelledPara id="lp-0006">
      <para>The gravity science scientific objectives are:</para>
      <sequentialList>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0014">To determine the masses of the asteroids visited</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0015">To measure the bulk density of Vesta and Ceres, in conjunction with topography, and determine heterogeneity</listItem>
        <listItem listItemPrefix="#" id="s-0016">To determine the gravitational fields of Vesta and Ceres</listItem>
      </sequentialList>
    </levelledPara>
